Our Story

His Story

Wendy and I met during Montreal Trek, one of the many international trips we took while at Harvard Business School. I was seated next to Wendy at dinner during the trip. I had a pleasant conversation with a cute girl with strong opinions and a strange, foreign accent that sounded vaguely British (hint: it's Singaporean). Back in Boston, we would occasionally see each other around campus. It was only once we were both in sunny San Francisco for summer internships that we got to know each other better! We hiked through Yosemite, enjoyed concerts in Golden Gate Park, cracked crab in the Marina, and even rode tramcars together through Lisbon. Through our shared experiences, I learned that there is much more to Wendy than your stereotypical finance nerd!

Her Story

I went on a Montreal Trek organized by a section mate during the first year of business school. I remember being seated next to Albert for dinner and I was doing most of the talking. (mostly complaining about school). Probably not a great first impression. A few weeks later, We ran into each other on campus and decided to grab dinner. I took a liking to him that night as i thought he was very smart, good mannered and humorous. But school was so busy that I didn't have a chance to catch up with him until one day I found out that we would both be in San Francisco for our summer internships. Having grown up in California and lived in the bay area, Albert gladly took the initiative to show me around during the summer. He initially lived at the outskirt of the city but would drive up frequently to have dinner/drinks with me. Right before my internship ended, i was casually mentioning that I would love to visit Yosemite but my summer was ending soon. I got a text message from him a couple of days later asking if i would like to go that weekend as he had organized a mini trip. I was very touched and thought to myself - " what a sweet and thoughtful guy!". And our spark continued from that day... The rest is history...

Resort at Squaw Creek

Our wedding venue is a AAA luxury resort at the base of Squaw Valley Ski Resort with Squaw Village and beautiful Lake Tahoe 5 minutes away. On the grounds, there is an 18-hole golf course, full-service spa, heated pools with jacuzzis and water slide, tennis courts, restaurants, bars and plenty of outdoor activities for families. Discounted room rates, $199 for a standard room or $229 for a fireplace suite, are available now for our wedding weekend with a 2-night minimum stay. Discounted valet parking, golf and spa rates are also available so be sure to mention the Wong-Dunn wedding at time of booking to get the discounted rates. Please book as soon as possible to ensure they will hold enough rooms for our party. Contact Ricky Bedger, Group Rooms coordinator: (530) 584-4089 or rbedger@destinationhotels.com

Village at Squaw Valley Lodging

If you would rather not stay at the resort, lodging is available nearby at Squaw Village only a couple minutes away with a free shuttle from the Resort at Squaw Creek. They have single rooms and one, two and three-bedroom condos available at reasonable rates. There are plenty of restaurants, shops, outdoor activities right in the village. Unfortunately, we can't offer discounted room rates should you choose to stay here. (888) 259-1428

Plumpjack Squaw Valley Inn

Our rehearsal dinner venue is a cozy, lodge-like boutique inn right at the base of Squaw Valley just outside the village. It includes a fine-dining restaurant with an extensive wine list, a casual bar menu in the lounge and a spa. Plumpjack are most known for their award-winning restaurants and wineries in Napa and San Francisco. Unfortunately, we can't offer discounted room rates here. (800) 323-7666

Travel

The Resort at Squaw Creek is located approximately 200 miles east of San Francisco, 100 miles east of Sacramento and 42 miles west of Reno.

For our guests traveling from out of state, the closest airport is Reno/Tahoe International (RNO), a little less than an hour away. From there, either rent a car or take the North Lake Tahoe Express shuttle to the resort. You could also fly into San Francisco International (SFO) and drive approximately 3.5 hours to Tahoe. It's a beautiful drive through the Sierras during the daytime and you will not need chains at this time of year.
